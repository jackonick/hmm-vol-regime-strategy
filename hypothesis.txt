Equity market volatility exhibits persistent regime behavior — extended periods of low, stable volatility followed by sharp transitions into high-volatility regimes driven by macro shocks, liquidity events, or shifts in market sentiment. These regimes are not random; they cluster in time and follow semi-predictable transition patterns. A Hidden Markov Model trained on VIX closing levels, daily VIX returns, and 20-day rolling volatility of VIX returns should be able to identify distinct volatility states and estimate the probability of transitioning between them. If the model can reliably detect when the market is shifting from a low-vol regime to a high-vol regime before the move fully materializes, this signal can be used to reduce equity exposure or position short volatility ahead of regime transitions. The strategy rebalances monthly, targeting consistent risk-adjusted returns with controlled drawdowns rather than maximizing raw performance — prioritizing Sharpe ratio and drawdown stability over total return.
